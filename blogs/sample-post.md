# How to Build a Diversified Portfolio

Diversification is the single most important concept in investing. It's the idea that you shouldn't put all your eggs in one basket — and when done right, it can reduce your portfolio's risk without sacrificing returns.

## Why Diversify?

Markets are unpredictable. A sector that outperforms one year may underperform the next. By spreading your investments across different asset classes, sectors, and geographies, you reduce the impact of any single investment's poor performance on your overall portfolio.

## The Core Building Blocks

A well-diversified portfolio typically includes a mix of:

- **Equity (Stocks/Equity Mutual Funds)** — for long-term growth
- **Debt (Bonds/Debt Mutual Funds)** — for stability and income
- **Gold or Commodities** — as a hedge against inflation
- **International Funds** — for geographic diversification

## A Simple Framework

For a moderate-risk investor, a common starting allocation might look like:

| Asset Class | Allocation |
|-------------|-----------|
| Large-Cap Equity | 40% |
| Mid/Small-Cap Equity | 20% |
| Debt/Bonds | 25% |
| Gold | 10% |
| International | 5% |

## Correlation Matters

The key to effective diversification isn't just owning many assets — it's owning assets that don't move in lockstep. This is where **correlation** comes in. Assets with low or negative correlation to each other provide the best diversification benefit.

Use tools like our Portfolio Analyzer to check the correlation between your funds before building your portfolio.

## Rebalancing

Over time, your portfolio will drift from its target allocation as different assets grow at different rates. Periodic rebalancing — say, every 6 to 12 months — brings it back in line and locks in gains from outperformers.

## Key Takeaways

1. Don't concentrate too heavily in any single fund or sector
2. Mix asset classes with low correlation
3. Rebalance periodically to maintain your target allocation
4. Use data-driven tools to validate your diversification

Happy investing!
